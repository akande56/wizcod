Our Activities

International Women's Day Celebration

March 8, 2024

We celebrated International Women's Day with a series of events highlighting the achievements of women in our communities and discussing the challenges they face. The day included panel discussions, workshops, and a community fair showcasing women-led businesses.

World Menstrual Hygiene Day

May 28, 2024

In collaboration with our partners, we organized awareness programs and distributed menstrual hygiene kits to teenage girls in our project areas. The event aimed to break the stigma surrounding menstruation and promote proper hygiene practices.

Graduation Ceremony for Livelihood Beneficiaries

December 15, 2023

We celebrated the graduation of our 2023 livelihood beneficiaries from the departments of catering, makeup and hairdressing, cosmotology, and tailoring. The ceremony showcased the skills acquired by the graduates and connected them with potential employers and mentors.

16 Days of Activism Against Gender-Based Violence

November 25 - December 10, 2023

Our team commemorated the 16 Days of Activism with a series of events, including workshops, community dialogues, and a march to raise awareness about gender-based violence. We collaborated with Women Peace Network members from our project implementing areas to maximize our impact.

International Day of Peace Celebration

September 21, 2023

We commemorated the International Day of Peace with a gathering of Women Peace Network members from our six project implementing areas: Bible Faith, Busa Buji/Rafin Pa, Naraguta/Kunga, Anguwan Jarawa Rusau, Sabon Layi, and Febuna. The event included peace-building workshops, cultural exchanges, and a community dialogue on conflict resolution strategies.

Monthly Women Peace Network Meetings

Ongoing (Monthly)

Our Women Peace Network holds regular monthly meetings to discuss local issues, share experiences, and plan community initiatives. These meetings foster collaboration and empower women to take active roles in peace-building and community development.

Engagement with Vigilante Group of Nigeria

Quarterly

We regularly engage with the Vigilante Group of Nigeria to promote community safety and improve relationships between security personnel and local women. These sessions focus on gender-sensitive security practices and community-based crime prevention strategies.

Adult Literacy Sessions in Babale

Ongoing (Weekly)

Our dedicated team conducts weekly adult literacy sessions in Babale, Jos North LGA of Plateau State. These classes focus on improving reading, writing, and basic numeracy skills, empowering women to participate more fully in their communities and pursue economic opportunities.

Advocacy Visits to Community Stakeholders

Ongoing

We regularly conduct advocacy visits to community stakeholders, including local government officials, traditional leaders, and religious authorities. These visits aim to build support for our programs, address women's issues, and promote gender equality in decision-making processes.

Livelihood Training Classes

Ongoing (Weekly)

Our foundation offers weekly training classes in catering, makeup and hairdressing, tailoring, and cosmotology. These classes provide women with valuable skills to start their own businesses or find employment, contributing to their economic empowerment.

Grant Distribution for Small-Scale Businesses

Quarterly

Every quarter, we distribute grants to selected women entrepreneurs to help them start or expand their small-scale businesses. This initiative aims to foster economic growth and independence among women in our communities.

Teenage Girls' Monthly Meetings

Ongoing (Monthly)

We conduct monthly meetings with teenage girls in our three project implementing areas: Babale Jos North, Mai Adiko Jos South, and Gindiri Mangu LGA, Plateau State. These meetings focus on menstrual health education, life skills development, and empowerment discussions.

Safe Emergency Delivery Caregivers' Meetings

Ongoing (Monthly)

Our Safe Emergency Delivery Caregivers program holds monthly meetings in Babale Jos North, Mai Adiko Jos South, and Gindiri Mangu LGA, Plateau State. These sessions provide ongoing training and support to community members who assist with emergency deliveries, ensuring safer childbirth experiences for women in remote areas.
